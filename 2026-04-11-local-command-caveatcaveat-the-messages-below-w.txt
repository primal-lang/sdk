6. MEDIUM — Parser falls through to primary() which will reject LetToken

What is wrong: The document says (line 36) that let cannot appear as a binary
operand: 1 + let x = 2 in x is invalid. This is correct but the mechanism is
unclear.

Why it is wrong: Looking at expression_parser.dart:286-306, primary() handles:
Boolean, Number, String, Identifier, (, [, {. If let is a keyword (LetToken),
and 1 + ... parses 1 then sees +, it calls the right operand which eventually
hits primary(). primary() will see LetToken and throw InvalidTokenError(peek)
— but this error message won't say "let expression not allowed here".

References:
- docs/roadmap/let.md:36 — states constraint
- lib/compiler/syntactic/expression_parser.dart:286-305 — primary() definition

Consequence: Error message will be generic ("Invalid token") rather than
helpful. User won't understand why let is rejected here.

Missing: Either explicit error handling in parser, or acceptance that
InvalidTokenError is sufficient.
